⛰️ Landslide Awareness Assessment

1. What is a landslide?

A water overflow event is commonly known as a flood.
A landslide is the movement of soil, rocks, and debris down a slope.
Strong wind is not considered a landslide.

2. Which is a common cause of landslides?

Heavy rainfall is one of the most common causes of landslides.
Snowfall is not the primary cause of most landslides.
Sunshine alone does not usually trigger landslides.

3. What should you do during a landslide?

You should move to a safe area immediately during a landslide.
Staying near the slope can increase the risk of injury.
Taking photos should not be your priority during a landslide.

4. Which area is safest during a landslide?

Areas near cliffs can be dangerous during a landslide.
Areas away from slopes and valleys are generally safer during a landslide.
River banks may not be safe during a landslide emergency.

5. Which warning sign may indicate a landslide?

Cracks appearing in the ground can be a warning sign of a landslide.
A clear sky is not a warning sign of a landslide.
Cool weather alone does not indicate a landslide.

6. What should be included in an emergency kit?

An emergency kit should contain food and drinking water.
Video games are not essential items in an emergency kit.
Toys alone are not sufficient for emergency preparedness.

7. Who provides official landslide warnings?

Government authorities provide official landslide warnings and updates.
Rumors should not be relied upon for landslide warnings.
Neighbors alone are not official sources of emergency warnings.

8. What should you avoid after a landslide?

Returning immediately to the affected area should be avoided after a landslide.
Listening to officials is important after a landslide event.
Staying alert helps ensure safety after a landslide.

9. Landslides can damage?

Landslides can damage roads, buildings, and other infrastructure.
Clouds are not damaged by landslides.
Sunlight cannot be damaged by a landslide.

10. What is the best action if authorities issue an evacuation order?

You should evacuate immediately when authorities issue an evacuation order.
Ignoring an evacuation order can put your safety at risk.
Waiting until danger arrives is not a safe decision.
