Introduction

A fire is a rapid chemical reaction that produces heat, light, and flames. It can spread quickly and cause severe damage to life, property, and environment. Fire accidents can occur in homes, industries, forests, and public places.

Causes of Fire

  • Electrical short circuits
  • Gas leaks
  • Unattended cooking
  • Smoking materials
  • Industrial accidents
  • Forest dry conditions

Effects of Fire

  • Loss of human lives
  • Destruction of buildings
  • Air pollution
  • Injury and burns
  • Damage to forests and wildlife
  • Economic loss

Fire Safety Before Fire

Install smoke detectors, check electrical wiring regularly, and keep fire extinguishers in important areas. Always store flammable materials safely.

Safety During Fire

  • Stay low to avoid smoke.
  • Use stairs, not lifts.
  • Cover nose with wet cloth.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Do not panic and run randomly.

Safety After Fire

  • Do not enter damaged buildings.
  • Check for injuries and get medical help.
  • Report gas leaks or electrical issues.
  • Follow authority instructions.

Conclusion

Fire safety awareness and quick action can save many lives. Knowing prevention and emergency steps helps reduce damage during fire disasters.
