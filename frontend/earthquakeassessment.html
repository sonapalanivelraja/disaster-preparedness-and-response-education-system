🌍 Advanced Earthquake Awareness Assessment

1. Which scale is commonly used to measure earthquake magnitude?

The Mercalli Scale is mainly used to measure the intensity and effects of an earthquake.
The Richter Scale is commonly used to measure earthquake magnitude.
The Celsius Scale is used to measure temperature, not earthquakes.

2. What is the point inside the Earth where an earthquake originates?

The epicenter is the point on the Earth's surface directly above the earthquake source.
A fault plane is the surface along which rocks move during an earthquake.
The focus, also called the hypocenter, is the point where an earthquake begins inside the Earth.

3. Which tectonic boundary is most associated with powerful earthquakes?

Convergent boundaries are often associated with powerful earthquakes.
Divergent boundaries usually create less powerful earthquakes.
Transform boundaries can also produce significant earthquakes.

4. Which seismic wave travels fastest through the Earth?

Surface waves travel along the Earth's surface and are generally slower.
P-Waves are the fastest seismic waves and arrive first.
S-Waves travel slower than P-Waves through the Earth.

5. Soil losing strength due to earthquake shaking is called?

Erosion is the gradual removal of soil by wind or water.
Weathering is the breakdown of rocks over time.
Liquefaction occurs when soil loses strength during earthquake shaking.

6. Earthquake magnitude measures what?

Earthquake magnitude measures the amount of energy released.
Building damage is not directly measured by earthquake magnitude.
The duration of shaking alone does not determine magnitude.

7. A strong undersea earthquake can trigger which disaster?

Tornadoes are not usually caused by undersea earthquakes.
A strong undersea earthquake can trigger a tsunami.
Droughts are not caused by earthquakes beneath the ocean.

8. What is the recommended action during strong indoor shaking?

Running to an elevator during an earthquake is unsafe.
Standing near windows can increase the risk of injury.
Drop, Cover, and Hold On is the recommended safety action.

9. Which instrument records seismic waves?

A seismograph is used to record seismic waves.
A thermometer is used to measure temperature.
A hygrometer is used to measure humidity in the air.

10. Smaller earthquakes that follow a major earthquake are called?

Foreshocks occur before the main earthquake event.
Aftershocks are smaller earthquakes that occur after a major earthquake.
Microbursts are weather-related events and not earthquakes.
